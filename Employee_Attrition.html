Employee Attrition Classification¶

Abstract¶

Employee attrition can have ruinous impact on the health of a company. Whenever an experience employee leaves the organization, there is a cost associated with it. A part of this cost is quantifiable numbers and the other part is hidden costs. The quantifiable cost includes the cost incurred in recruiting a replacement and training them. It would be great to have a machine learning model that will help the management to identify the employees who may potentially leave or quit. So that the management can take corrective measures to avoid employee attrition. The intend of the project is to use different classification models and tools available to accurately predict whether an employee will leave the company or not. The various classification techniques used in this project are Logistic Regression classifier, Support vector Machine classifier, Neural Networks and Ensemble methods (Decision Forests). The tools used are python in Jupyter note book and Microsoft Azure Machine Learning

Introduction¶

High employee attrition rate is a bad news for any organization. Employee turnover rate is number of employees who left during the entire year divided by the total number of employees at the beginning of the year and it is calculated annually. A high employee turnover may be risky for health of the company as high cost are associate with it. The quantifiable cost includes the cost involves recruiting, training etc. Generally, the productivity of the new hire is lower until he/she learn the business. For a customer facing role, if some customer/s are unhappy with the new hire, the business could lose customers who are dissatisfied with service leading to a loss in the revenue. Thus, it is very crucial for companys management to understand the factors leading to employee attrition and to identify the potential employees who could quit and finally to institutionalized measures to reduce attrition rates.

There can be umpteen reasons for employee attrition. Many of these reasons are personal and may varies from person to person, it can be as diverse as different human personalities. But it is important to analyze some of the basic factors that triggers most resignations?

As discussed, the primary goal of this project is to correctly identify the potential employees in a company who could leave using a classification model.

Reasons for Employee Attrition¶

To take corrective measure to stop high attrition rate in an organization, it is important to analyze what are the factors that influences a persons decision to quite the company. Here are some of the reasons, why an employee might leave his/her current job :-

  1. Mostly people leave their employer for an increased paycheck or career advancement,
  2. Many researches also shows that often the search for a new position is trigger due to dissatisfaction with an immediate manager.
  3. Employee are not engaged and they are bored. High-performing workers need to feel that they are being challenged and are moving forward in terms of professional growth and development.

# Creates boxplots on the data
def EA_snsbox(df,col):
    import matplotlib
    import numpy as np
    import seaborn as sns
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    sns.set_style("dark")
    g=sns.boxplot(x="Attrition",y=col,data= df,palette="Set1")
    sns.plt.title('Box plot of ' + col + '\n for Employee Attrition')
    g.set_xticklabels(labels=df["Attrition"], rotation=90)
    
# creates the bar charts on the data
def EA_bar(df, cols):
    import seaborn as sns
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    pf=df.groupby([cols,"Attrition"]).size().reset_index(name='counts')
    sns.set_style("dark")
    g=sns.barplot(x=cols, y="counts",hue="Attrition", data=pf)
    sns.plt.title(cols +' vs. \n Employee Attrition')
    sns.plt.ylabel('Count')
    g.set_xticklabels(labels=np.unique(pf[cols]), rotation=90)

3. Support Vector Machine Classifer¶

The class used for SVM classification in scikit-learn is svm.SVC(). The parameter for SVM classifier:

  1. C: It is the regularization parameter, C, of the error term.
  2. kernel: It specifies the kernel type to be used in the algorithm. It can be linear, poly, rbf, sigmoid, precomputed, or a callable. The default value is rbf'.
  3. degree: It is the degree of the polynomial kernel function (poly) and is ignored by all other kernels. The default value is 3.
  4. gamma: It is the kernel coefficient for rbf, poly, and sigmoid. If gamma is auto, then 1/n_features will be used instead.

The SVM is tune by changing parameters C, gamma and the kernel function. The function for tuning the parameters is called gridSearchCV(). param_grid: It is the dictionary or list with parameters names (string) as keys and lists of parameter settings to try as values.
